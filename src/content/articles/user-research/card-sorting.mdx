Information Architecture (IA) is the structural design of shared information environments. When building a website with dozens or hundreds of pages, deciding how to group those pages in the navigation menu is a daunting task. Card sorting is a proven user research technique designed to solve exactly this problem.

In a card sorting session, participants are given a set of cards, each representing a piece of content or a feature. They are asked to group the cards together in a way that makes sense to them. In an "open" card sort, participants also invent the names for the categories they create. This reveals how users naturally categorize your content and the vocabulary they use to describe it.

By analyzing the patterns across multiple participants, designers can build a navigation structure that mirrors the user's mental model, rather than relying on internal company jargon. If users consistently expect to find "Return Policy" under "Customer Support" rather than "Legal," the navigation should reflect that expectation to prevent frustration.
