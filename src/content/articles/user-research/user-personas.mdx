User personas are fictional characters created to represent the different user types that might use a service, product, site, or brand in a similar way. When used correctly, they are a powerful tool to build empathy and keep the team focused on the end-user.

The most common mistake teams make is inventing personas based on gut feelings in a conference room. To be effective, personas must be forged from qualitative and quantitative research. You need to conduct interviews, analyze surveys, and observe real users before synthesizing that data into an archetype.

Furthermore, demographics like "35-year-old married accountant" are rarely as useful as behavioral traits. Knowing that a user "feels overwhelmed by complex dashboards and prioritizes speed above all else" directly informs design decisions in a way that their age and occupation do not.

A well-crafted persona acts as a north star. Whenever a team disagrees on a feature, they simply ask, "What would our persona need?"
