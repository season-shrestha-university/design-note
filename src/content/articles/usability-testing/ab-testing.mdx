A/B testing, also known as split testing, is a method of comparing two versions of a webpage or app against each other to determine which one performs better. In usability testing, it is the ultimate arbiter of truth.

When designers debate whether a primary action button should be green or blue, or whether a checkout form should be one page or two, opinions often clash. A/B testing solves this by showing version A to 50% of users and version B to the other 50%. The data then clearly reveals which version leads to higher engagement or conversion rates.

To run an effective A/B test, it is crucial to isolate variables. If you change the headline, the button color, and the layout all at once, you will never know which change actually impacted the results.

Data-driven design doesn't replace intuition; it validates it.
