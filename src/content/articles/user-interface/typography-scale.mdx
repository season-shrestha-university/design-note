Typography is the backbone of user interface design. Because the vast majority of digital content is text, the way that text is sized and structured directly dictates the usability and aesthetic appeal of a product. Choosing font sizes at random inevitably leads to a disjointed, messy interface.

To create visual harmony, designers use a typographic scale. Similar to musical scales, a typographic scale is a progression of font sizes derived from a base size (usually 16px) multiplied by a specific mathematical ratio (such as 1.25 for a Major Third scale). This generates a set of sizes that relate to one another proportionally.

When H1, H2, H3, and body copy all adhere to this mathematical rhythm, the interface inherently feels balanced. It establishes a clear, predictable visual hierarchy, allowing users to scan the page quickly and instantly understand the relationship between different sections of content. Good typography is invisible to the user, but its absence is glaring.
