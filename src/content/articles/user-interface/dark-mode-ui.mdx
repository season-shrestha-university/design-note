Dark mode has transitioned from a niche developer preference to a standard feature expected in nearly every modern application. But designing a good dark mode interface is far more complex than simply inverting colors.

The primary goal of dark mode is to reduce eye strain, especially in low-light environments. However, using a pure black (#000000) background combined with pure white text creates a contrast ratio that is too severe, leading to visual fatigue and a "halation" effect where the text appears to bleed into the background.

Instead, UI designers use dark grays (like #121212) as the foundational surface color. This softer contrast is much gentler on the eyes. Furthermore, colors interact differently with dark surfaces. A vibrant blue that looks great on white may appear neon and jarring on dark gray. Designers must use desaturated, lighter shades of their primary colors to maintain accessibility and aesthetic harmony.

When executed properly, a dark theme feels elegant, immersive, and comfortable.
