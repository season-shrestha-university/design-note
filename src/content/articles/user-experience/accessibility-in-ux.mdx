Accessibility in User Experience (UX) means creating digital products that are usable by everyone, regardless of their visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive abilities. Far too often, it is treated as an afterthought or a compliance checklist at the end of a project.

In reality, inclusive design is the hallmark of excellent UX. When we ensure sufficient color contrast for users with visual impairments, we also help users trying to read a screen in bright sunlight. When we provide captions for videos for the deaf community, we also help a commuter watching a video on a noisy train without headphones.

Key practices include using semantic HTML so screen readers can navigate effectively, ensuring interactive elements are large enough to be easily tapped, and never relying on color alone to convey critical information.

By designing for the margins, we inherently create a more robust, intuitive, and accessible experience for the center.
