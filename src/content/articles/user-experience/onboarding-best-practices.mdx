The onboarding experience is the most critical phase of a user's journey. It is the bridge between a user signing up and a user actually achieving success with your product. A poorly designed onboarding flow leads directly to high churn rates, no matter how good the underlying software is.

A common mistake is treating onboarding like an instruction manual. Forcing users to click through a 10-step tooltip tour before they can interact with the app is overwhelming and easily forgotten. Instead, the best onboarding experiences are contextual and interactive. They guide the user to perform a meaningful action immediately, such as creating their first project or sending their first message.

Furthermore, empty states should be utilized as onboarding tools. Instead of showing a blank screen that says "No files found," an empty state should contain a clear call-to-action button and a brief explanation of how to get started. By focusing on immediate value delivery, users build confidence and form a habit around the product.
