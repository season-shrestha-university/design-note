Social share buttons have been a common feature on blogs and websites for years, allowing users to share content directly to platforms such as Facebook, X, and LinkedIn. However, user behavior has changed, and these buttons are no longer widely used.

Modern users usually share content by copying links or using built-in sharing features on their devices. These methods provide more flexibility because users can choose where and how they want to share the content.

From a UX perspective, websites should move away from adding multiple social icons and focus on simpler sharing interactions. A Copy Link button and a native Share option better match current user expectations while reducing clutter, performance issues, and unnecessary complexity.
