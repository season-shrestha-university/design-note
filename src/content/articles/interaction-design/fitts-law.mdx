Fitts’s Law is a predictive model of human movement primarily used in human-computer interaction and ergonomics. It states that the time it takes to move to a target area is a function of the distance to the target and the size of the target.

In practical UI design, this means two things: make important buttons larger, and place them closer to where the user's cursor or thumb already is. This is why floating action buttons (FABs) on mobile devices are placed in the bottom right corner, perfectly positioned for right-handed thumb reach.

Furthermore, Fitts's Law highlights the value of screen edges. On a desktop computer, moving the mouse to the very top or bottom of the screen requires almost no precision because the cursor stops at the edge. This makes top navigation bars and bottom docks incredibly fast to access.
